# House Eleven Motion System

> Motion should never announce itself.
>
> It should quietly guide attention.

---

# Philosophy

Motion is part of the storytelling language of House Eleven.

It is not decoration.

Every animation exists to support meaning, establish rhythm or reinforce hierarchy.

If motion becomes the centre of attention, it has failed.

The visitor should remember the feeling, not the animation.

---

# Core Principles

- Calm over dramatic
- Cinematic over playful
- Intentional over excessive
- Elegant over impressive
- Consistent over surprising

---

# Motion Goals

Motion exists to:

- Guide attention
- Reveal information
- Reinforce hierarchy
- Improve continuity
- Create rhythm

Motion should never exist simply because something can move.

---

# Animation Philosophy

House Eleven animations should feel:

- Soft
- Natural
- Effortless
- Restrained
- Premium

Animations should resemble breathing rather than performance.

---

# Timing

Fast interactions should feel responsive.

Large transitions should feel deliberate.

Avoid abrupt starts and stops.

The visitor should never feel rushed.

---

# Easing

Prefer smooth easing curves.

Avoid mechanical linear movement.

Acceleration and deceleration should feel organic.

Every animation should settle naturally.

---

# Scroll Behaviour

Scrolling is part of the experience.

Sections should flow into one another.

Visitors should never feel like they are jumping between disconnected blocks.

Scrolling should resemble turning pages rather than navigating software.

---

# Page Transitions

Page transitions should reinforce continuity.

Visitors should feel they remain inside the same House.

Avoid dramatic scene changes.

Subtlety creates confidence.

---

# Hover Behaviour

Hover effects should communicate responsiveness.

Avoid exaggerated scaling.

Avoid unnecessary colour changes.

Small movement often communicates quality more effectively.

---

# Image Motion

Photography should remain the focus.

Motion should support imagery rather than compete with it.

Images may reveal themselves gradually.

They should never distract from themselves through excessive movement.

---

# Text Motion

Typography should enter gracefully.

Large headings deserve time.

Supporting content should follow naturally.

Hierarchy should exist in motion as well as layout.

---

# Navigation Motion

Navigation should feel dependable.

Changes in transparency, blur and visibility should feel smooth and predictable.

Visitors should never wonder what the navigation is doing.

---

# Cursor Motion

Cursor interactions should feel lightweight.

The cursor should respond immediately without becoming distracting.

Every cursor state should communicate purpose.

---

# Reduced Motion

The experience should remain complete even when motion is reduced.

Animations should simplify gracefully.

Accessibility is part of luxury.

---

# Performance

Smooth motion is more important than complex motion.

Dropped frames damage trust.

Every animation should justify its rendering cost.

---

# Motion Review Checklist

Before introducing any animation, ask:

Does it guide attention?

Does it strengthen the story?

Could it be simpler?

Would removing it improve the experience?

If the answer to the final question is yes, remove it.

---

# Final Principle

The best motion is often the motion people never consciously notice.

They simply remember how calm the experience felt.
